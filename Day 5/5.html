ॐ स्कन्दमात्रै नम:  

HER SON :

Skandamata is depicted holding her son, Lord Skanda (Kartikeya), in her lap. This signifies motherly love and protection.

SIX HEAD :

Lord Skanda is often portrayed with six heads, symbolizing his six attributes - • wisdom • strength • fame wealth
• dispassion • divine knowledge.

LOTUS IN HER HAND :

She holds a lotus flower in one hand, symbolizing purity and auspiciousness.

PRAYER POSTURE
(Abhaya Mudra) :

Her other hand is raised in a gesture of assurance, granting blessings and protection to her devotees.

LION AS HER VEHICLE :

Skandamata rides a lion,
representing courage,
power, and fearlessness.

SIGNIFICANCE :

Worshipping on Navratri is believed to bring strength, courage, and protection from negative energies. It's an opportunity to invoke maternal love and divine blessings.
