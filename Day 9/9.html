ॐ देवी सिद्धिदात्र्यै नमः

Divine Goddess  Siddhidatri

Siddhidatri Devi is the ninth and final form of Goddess Durga, celebrated on the last day of Navratri. She is the bestower of supernatural powers and blessings, and her name means "the giver of siddhis," which are spiritual and mystical abilities.

DEVI

Iconic Image

Siddhidatri Devi is depicted with four arms, seated on a lotus, and often holds a mace, a discus, a conch shell, and a lotus flower. Her radiant appearance symbolizes purity and divinity.

SPECIALITY

Granting Siddhis

The Goddess is believed to fulfill the devotees' wishes and bestow them with siddhis or special abilities. These siddhis can be physical, mental, or spiritual in nature, and they help individuals on their spiritual journey.

BELIEVE

Navratri Worship

During the auspicious festival of Navratri, Siddhidatri Devi is worshipped with great devotion on the ninth day. Devotees fast, chant mantras, and offer prayers to seek her blessings for prosperity, wisdom, and spiritual growth.

WORSHIP

    Spiritual    Significance

Siddhidatri Devi represents the culmination of the Navratri celebrations, signifying the attainment of self-realization and ultimate spiritual fulfillment. Her grace empowers individuals to overcome obstacles and achieve success in their endeavors.
